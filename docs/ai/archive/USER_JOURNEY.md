# USER JOURNEY & FLOWS

## 1. Onboarding
- **Goal:** Comprehensively capture the essential variables without overwhelming the user.
- **Inputs:** Basic details, training history, training frequency (days per week), and exact equipment access.
- **Outcome:** This data *immediately* and tangibly shapes the generated program. The user should feel that their specific answers resulted in a bespoke architectural plan.

## 2. The First Week (Progressive Profiling)
- The user is presented with their "Benchmark Week".
- Rather than asking for estimated 1RMs or exhaustive volume limits during the onboarding screens, the app uses their first few workouts (cardio and weights) to collect real-world benchmarking data.
- The app then "updates" their profile behind the scenes based on this verified data.

## 3. The Daily Workout Experience
- **Pre-Workout:** User opens the app, sees their prescribed workout flawlessly formatted. They have the option to swap an exercise (e.g., swapping Barbell Squat for Hack Squat) and the AI adjusts the target loads/reps immediately.
- **Intra-Workout:** Logging must be frictionless. Big, legible numbers. Easy input for Reps and RIR/RPE. The user can see historical data for that exact lift instantly.
- **Post-Workout:** A beautiful summary screen highlighting tonnage moved, volume landmarks hit, and a sleek chart of their progress.

## 4. The Weekly Review (The "Coach" Interaction)
- At the end of the microcycle, the user receives an elegant "Weekly Review".
- The AI (Anthropic backend) presents a short, human-readable insight: *"You hit failure earlier than expected on pressing movements this week. I'm slightly reducing your pressing volume for Week 3 to manage fatigue."*
- The user feels seen and coached by a high-end intelligence.
